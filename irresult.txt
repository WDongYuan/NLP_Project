Dempsey studied the play of Argentina's national team, especially Diego Maradona, and was heartbroken when the news came to Nacogdoches that Maradona would not be playing in the 1994 World Cup game played in the Cotton Bowl.
He won the highest individual honor in football in America when he was named Honda Player of the Year for 2006, beating Fulham teammates Kasey Keller and Brian McBride in a poll of sportswriters.
Dempsey scored his first goal for Fulham on 5 May when he struck the only goal of a 10 home win over Liverpool.
On June 12, 2010, Dempsey became the second American (after Brian McBride) to score in more than one World Cup when he scored the equalizer goal against England in the Americans' first game of the 2010 World Cup after the England goalkeeper Robert Green made a major error.
Dempsey was called offside, but the replay showed that he was most likely not offside.
On May 2, 2006, Dempsey was named to the US roster for the 2006 World Cup in Germany.
Dempsey was voted Fulham player of the season for 201011 by the Fulham fans, and his 12 Premier League strikes meant he was the club's leading goalscorer for the campaign.
